Cheap vs Affordable — The Critical Difference

Cheap means low price achieved by reducing quality. Template graphics. No vector source files. No commercial rights. Work that fails in real-world use within 12 months. The hidden cost of cheap logo design is not the initial price — it is the rebrand you will need to pay for when the cheap logo fails to scale to print, cannot be used on merchandise, or is identical to a competitor's logo because you both used the same template.

Affordable means low price achieved by reducing overhead — not quality. A highly skilled designer working efficiently through an optimised platform, without the account management costs, studio rent, and brand strategy fees of a traditional agency. The design skill and output quality are equivalent. The price difference is structural, not qualitative. Fiverr Pro is the most prominent example of this model working at scale.

The Test

Ask any logo designer this before paying: "Can I see the vector source files after delivery, and will I own full commercial rights in writing?" A professional answers yes immediately. A template seller hesitates, deflects, or charges extra. This question alone filters out every logo that will fail you in real-world use.

Red Flags That Signal a Cheap Logo

⚠ Never commission a logo from a designer who does any of these:

  • Delivers only JPEG or PNG — no vector source files included or available
  • Cannot confirm commercial rights in writing — "you can use it" is not a commercial rights statement
  • Uses stock icon libraries as the primary element — your "unique" logo is shared with thousands of others
  • Delivers within minutes of ordering — original design requires time; instant delivery means template retrieval
  • Charges for each revision on a basic package — professional logos include revision rounds as standard
  • Asks you to pay in full upfront with no escrow or protection mechanism
  • Has no verifiable portfolio of original work — stock marketplace or template gallery screenshots don't count

What to Always Demand at Any Budget

  • Vector source files — AI, EPS, or SVG. Non-negotiable. Without these, the logo cannot be used professionally.
  • Commercial usage rights in writing — you own it outright, no ongoing fees, no licensing restrictions.
  • Black and white version — proves the design works without colour dependency and enables mono print applications.
  • At least one revision round — original work requires refinement; no-revision packages are a warning sign.
  • Payment protection — escrow or equivalent. Your money should not be released until you approve the work.
