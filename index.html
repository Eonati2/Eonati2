The Problem

What a Bad Logo Is
Actually Costing You

Most business owners think a logo is just a visual. It isn't. Every time a potential customer sees your logo, it sends a signal about every single other thing you do. A weak logo doesn't just look bad — it actively reduces trust, raises price sensitivity, and costs you conversions before you've said a word.

👁
You lose the first impression — in under 90ms
The human brain forms a judgement about brand trustworthiness in less than a second. A generic or amateurish logo signals low quality instantly — before the customer reads your offer, your reviews, or your pricing.
💸
You attract low-budget clients and lose high-value ones
Your visual identity sets the anchor for how customers value your services. A cheap-looking logo makes premium pricing feel wrong — even when the service is excellent. Your logo is literally setting your price ceiling.
🔄
You'll pay twice — redesigning in 12–24 months
Logos built on templates or poor foundations don't scale. They fail on merchandise, print, and signage. Most businesses that cut corners on a logo end up paying 2–3x total for the redesign they should have done first.

Yes, significantly. Standard Fiverr is open to anyone — quality ranges from excellent to unusable. Fiverr Pro requires a manual application and vetting process. Fewer than 1% of applicants are approved. Pro designers have verified portfolios, professional credentials, and are held to a higher standard of communication and delivery. Is it safe to pay a logo designer on Fiverr?
Yes. All Fiverr Pro payments are held in escrow until you approve the delivered work. You review every file before any money is released to the designer. If the work doesn't match the brief, you can request revisions or a refund through Fiverr's resolution centre. This protection is not available with most agencies or independent freelancers.
