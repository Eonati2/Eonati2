THE SCIENCE OF WHAT MAKES PINTEREST CONTENT VIRAL
(these are not suggestions — they are laws. apply every single time)
═══════════════════════════════════════════════════════

LAW 1 — THE 0.3-SECOND RULE
You have 0.3 seconds before a thumb scrolls past. The image must communicate 
a COMPLETE idea before the text is read. If the image needs explanation, it fails.
Application: Image text overlay must carry the full hook. Description amplifies it.

LAW 2 — KEYWORDS ARE THE ALGORITHM
Pinterest is a visual search engine, not a social network. Treat every title and 
description like a Google meta description. Front-load keywords in the first 7 words.
Top keywords for this niche (2025 Pinterest search volume):
HIGH: "logo design," "logo designer," "brand identity," "small business branding"
HIGH: "logo color," "professional logo," "branding tips," "business logo ideas"  
MED: "Fiverr Pro," "logo design cost," "minimalist logo," "startup branding"
MED: "personal brand logo," "logo redesign," "brand colors," "logo psychology"
LOW/NICHE: "fashion logo," "restaurant logo," "medical logo," "real estate logo"
Rule: Use 2–3 high-volume keywords naturally in the first sentence.

LAW 3 — SAVES ARE THE SIGNAL
Pinterest rewards saves over clicks. A pin saved = Pinterest shows it to more people.
Save triggers: education, value, "I'll need this later," checklists, templates, surprises.
Application: Every description must give a reason to SAVE before it gives a reason to CLICK.

LAW 4 — THE CURIOSITY LOOP
The most viral pins open a loop the image cannot close. The answer is only at the link.
Wrong: "Here are 5 logo tips" (complete — no reason to click)
Right: "There's one color combination that makes logos look instantly cheap — 
most business owners use it without knowing" (loop open — must click to find out)

LAW 5 — SPECIFICITY BUILDS TRUST
Specific claims convert. Vague claims disappear.
Wrong: "Affordable logo design that looks professional"
Right: "A Fiverr Pro logo designer for $150 — here's exactly what you get"

LAW 6 — EMOTION FIRST, LOGIC SECOND
People decide emotionally then justify logically. Lead with how they'll FEEL when 
their brand looks right. Follow with the rational case (price, quality, process).

LAW 7 — THE IDENTITY BRIDGE
The most powerful Pinterest content sells an identity, not a product.
Not: "Get a professional logo"
But: "Build the brand that makes people take you seriously before you say a word"

LAW 8 — PINTEREST SEO STRUCTURE
Title: Primary keyword + hook (max 100 characters)
Description: Keyword sentence 1 → Value/story sentences 2-4 → CTA sentence 5
Alt text: Keyword-rich, descriptive, different from title (max 500 characters)
Hashtags: 5–8 only. Mix broad (#LogoDesign) + niche (#StartupBranding) + intent (#HireADesigner)

LAW 9 — THE PREMIUM VISUAL CODE
High-converting pins in the design niche follow these visual rules:
- Vertical: 1000×1500px or 1080×1920px (2:3 ratio)
- Maximum 3 colors. Usually: 1 dominant + 1 accent + white or black
- Typography-first: the text IS the design, not decoration on a photo
- High contrast: either very light background/dark text OR very dark/light text
- One dominant headline at the top third, subtext at bottom third
- Never use gradients as backgrounds — they compress poorly and look amateur
- Clean whitespace — premium design breathes, it doesn't crowd

LAW 10 — THE CALL TO ACTION RULE
CTAs that work: "See the full breakdown →" / "Find your match →" / "Take the quiz →"
CTAs that fail: "Click here" / "Check it out" / "Learn more" (too generic, too passive)
The CTA must promise a specific outcome, not a generic action.
