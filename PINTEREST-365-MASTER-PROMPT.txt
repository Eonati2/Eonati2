SECTION 2 — THE 13 PSYCHOLOGY FRAMEWORKS (rotate through these automatically)
━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━

Every pin must be built on one of these psychological triggers. Choose based on 
the day number (Day % 13 gives you the framework index 0–12):

FRAMEWORK 0 — CURIOSITY GAP
Open a loop the reader must close by clicking. Never answer the question in the pin.
Example angle: "There's one mistake almost every first-time business owner makes with 
their logo — and it costs them clients for years. Here's what it is."
Best for: High save rate, drives clicks to close the loop.
Conversion type: Awareness → Consideration

FRAMEWORK 1 — PAIN AGITATION SOLUTION (PAS)
Name the pain → make it hurt more → offer the solution.
Example angle: "Your logo looks amateur. Clients can tell in 3 seconds. And once they 
decide, they don't come back. Here's how to fix it today for less than $200."
Best for: Small business owners who feel their brand isn't working.
Conversion type: Direct — high commercial intent.

FRAMEWORK 2 — SOCIAL PROOF + AUTHORITY
Use numbers, credentials, rankings, and results.
Example angle: "I reviewed 200+ Fiverr logo designers so you don't have to. 
These 3 rose to the top. Ranked by quality, communication, and ROI."
Best for: Trust building. Pinterest users save for future reference.
Conversion type: Research → Purchase (higher ticket)

FRAMEWORK 3 — BEFORE/AFTER TRANSFORMATION
Show the gap between where they are and where they could be.
Example angle: "Before: a generic logo from Canva that blends into every competitor. 
After: a custom mark that makes clients choose you on sight."
Best for: Aspirational saves. Triggers imagination and desire.
Conversion type: Aspiration → Action

FRAMEWORK 4 — FEAR + LOSS AVERSION
People fear loss 2x more than they desire gain (Kahneman). Make inaction feel costly.
Example angle: "Every month you operate with a low-quality logo is money left on the 
table. First impressions happen in 0.05 seconds — and don't get second chances."
Best for: Reluctant buyers who know they need this but keep delaying.
Conversion type: Urgency-driven purchase

FRAMEWORK 5 — SCARCITY + SPECIFICITY
Limited availability, specific numbers, real constraints.
Example angle: "This Fiverr Pro designer books out 6 weeks in advance. 
She only takes 4 new clients per month. Here's how to get on her list."
Best for: Drives immediate clicks. Breaks the 'save and forget' cycle.
Conversion type: Impulse → Direct conversion

FRAMEWORK 6 — EDUCATION + VALUE BOMB
Give so much value in the pin description that clicking feels like getting MORE.
Example angle: "5 things every professional logo must do (that most designers skip):
1. Work in black and white. 2. Scale to 16px without losing detail. 3. [etc]"
Best for: Highest save rate on Pinterest. These get re-pinned for years.
Conversion type: Trust → Long-tail conversion

FRAMEWORK 7 — COMPARISON + CONTRAST
Put two options side-by-side. Make the right choice obvious.
Example angle: "Fiverr Pro vs. design agency: I tested both for my logo. 
Here's what I got, what it cost, and which one I'd choose again."
Best for: Mid-funnel — people already researching.
Conversion type: Decision-stage conversion (highest value)

FRAMEWORK 8 — PERSONALIZATION + QUIZ/ASSESSMENT
Make the reader feel the content was made specifically for them.
Example angle: "Not sure which logo style fits your brand? Answer 5 questions 
and get a personalized recommendation — including the exact designer to hire."
Best for: Quiz page. Highest conversion rate of all 28 pages.
Conversion type: Committed buyer (quiz completers convert at 3x baseline)

FRAMEWORK 9 — ASPIRATIONAL IDENTITY
Sell the identity, not the product. Who do they become?
Example angle: "The logo your clients see before they ever speak to you. 
Make it say exactly what you want."
Best for: Personal brands, fashion, luxury, consultants.
Conversion type: Brand-driven purchase (higher ticket buyers)

FRAMEWORK 10 — PATTERN INTERRUPT
Start with something unexpected. Violate assumptions.
Example angle: "Stop using blue in your professional service logo. 
Every lawyer, accountant, and financial advisor already does. Here's what to use instead."
Best for: Stops the scroll. High impression-to-save rate.
Conversion type: Curiosity → Click

FRAMEWORK 11 — SPECIFICITY + DATA
Specific numbers are always more credible than general claims.
Example angle: "A professional logo increases business credibility by 48% 
(according to a Nielsen study). Here's how to get one for under $300."
Best for: Analytical buyers, B2B audience.
Conversion type: Reasoned purchase

FRAMEWORK 12 — COMMITMENT + MOMENTUM
Small yes leads to bigger yes (Cialdini commitment principle).
Example angle: "You've already decided your brand needs to look better. 
The only question is who to hire. Here are the top 3 choices — all pre-vetted."
Best for: Warm audience who already knows they need this.
Conversion type: Final push to purchase
