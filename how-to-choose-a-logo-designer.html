The 6-Step Selection Framework

1

Define your brand category

Before you look at a single portfolio, identify your brand's primary category. Are you a tech startup or SaaS product (needs clean, minimal, innovation-forward design)? A corporation or enterprise (needs authority, credibility, system-thinking)? A luxury or premium product brand (needs refinement, restraint, typographic precision)? This single decision eliminates 80% of wrong choices.

2

Set a budget range before you browse

Decide your budget before looking at portfolios so you are not swayed by work you cannot afford or undershooting on work that would genuinely serve your brand better. Professional logo design starts at €23 on Fiverr Pro. A full brand kit is €99–€175. Know your number before you start.

3

Review portfolios for niche alignment — not just aesthetic preference

Look for designers whose portfolio contains work similar to what you need — in the same industry, style category, and complexity level. A portfolio full of luxury skincare brands does not tell you how a designer will perform on a SaaS brief, even if all the work looks beautiful. Filter for relevance, not just quality.

4

Check verified, independent reviews

Only trust reviews you can independently verify. Fiverr Pro reviews are attached to real completed orders, verified by a third party, and publicly readable. Look for volume, consistency, and recency. A designer with 9,000+ reviews at 4.8 stars gives you far more confidence than an agency with 15 hand-picked case studies on their own website.

5

Confirm deliverable formats before ordering

Always confirm that you will receive true vector files (AI, EPS, or SVG). If a designer only delivers PNG or JPEG, you are not receiving a professional logo — you are receiving a rasterized image that cannot be scaled without quality loss. All Fiverr Pro designers on Eonati include full vector deliverables as standard.

6

Place your order with payment protection

Use a platform with escrow payment protection. On Fiverr Pro, your payment is held securely and only released to the designer when you approve the final work. This protects your investment throughout the process. Never pay a designer in full upfront without this protection in place.

Why Niche Alignment Matters More Than Rating or Price

The single most common logo design mistake is choosing a designer based on rating or price alone, while ignoring niche fit. A designer with a ★4.9 rating who specialises in luxury beauty brands will produce the wrong output for a B2B SaaS company — even at their absolute best.

Design aesthetics are domain-specific. The visual language of a luxury skincare brand and a venture-backed tech startup are fundamentally different — different typography, different use of space, different color logic, different compositional principles. A designer who has internalised one domain's language will instinctively apply it to any brief, regardless of what you ask for.

Most Common Expensive Mistake

Hiring a luxury or fashion designer for a SaaS product (or vice versa). The output will look beautiful but communicate entirely the wrong message to your audience. Niche alignment is non-negotiable.

How to Write a Brief That Gets Great Results

The quality of your brief is the single biggest variable in the quality of the output you receive. A strong brief does not mean a long one — it means a precise one. Here is the template:

Logo Design Brief Template

Company name and what it does
Example: "Luminary — a SaaS platform that helps e-commerce brands manage inventory across multiple warehouses."
Target audience
Example: "Operations managers at mid-size e-commerce brands (50–500 employees). Values: efficiency, reliability, clarity."
Style direction
Example: "Minimal, modern, tech-forward. Clean lines. No gradients. Think Linear.app, Notion, Vercel."
Colors (include AND exclude)
Example: "Prefer deep navy or slate. Accent can be electric blue or teal. Avoid green (competitor owns it), avoid orange."
Reference logos you admire (and why)
Example: "Stripe logo — love the geometric precision and the diagonal. Figma — love how the mark works as an app icon."
Required deliverables
Example: "Primary logo, horizontal lockup, icon-only version for app use, dark and light mode variants. AI, EPS, SVG, and PNG."

5 Mistakes to Avoid When Hiring a Logo Designer

  1. Choosing based on price alone: The cheapest option is often not the worst — but making price the only criterion means you will not filter for niche alignment, and the output will likely miss the mark for your brand category.
  2. Not confirming vector file delivery upfront: Always confirm AI, EPS, or SVG delivery before placing your order. If a designer only provides PNG, you cannot scale the logo for print, merchandise, or large-format use.
  3. Giving no style references: "Make it look professional" is not a brief. Give specific reference logos, visual styles, and color directions. The more precise your input, the more precisely the designer can serve your brand.
  4. Hiring a luxury designer for a tech brand (or vice versa): Aesthetic niche alignment is more important than any other factor. A great designer in the wrong category will produce the wrong output.
  5. Paying without escrow protection: Never pay in full upfront without a payment protection mechanism in place. Fiverr Pro's escrow system ensures your money is only released when you approve the final work.

Frequently Asked Questions

How do I know if a logo designer is good?
Look for three things: niche-relevant portfolio work (does their existing work look like what your brand needs?), volume of independent verified reviews (not testimonials on their own site), and clear vector file deliverables (AI, EPS, SVG). All Fiverr Pro designers on Eonati meet all three criteria.
How many concepts should I expect to receive?
Most Fiverr Pro packages include 2–5 initial concepts, with revisions included after your feedback. The number of revision rounds varies by package tier. Read the package description before ordering so you know exactly what is included — and choose a higher tier if you anticipate needing more exploration.
What if the designer doesn't understand my brief?
On Fiverr Pro, you can message the designer before placing your order to discuss your brief and ensure alignment. This is always recommended for complex or niche-specific briefs. If the first concepts miss the mark, you have revisions included to redirect the designer. Your payment stays in escrow until you are satisfied.
Is it better to hire one designer or run a design contest?
For most businesses, hiring a specialist directly produces better results than a contest. Contest-based platforms (99designs, DesignCrowd) incentivize designers to submit generic, safe concepts because they are competing without certainty of payment. A vetted specialist who understands your brief will invest more care and produce more tailored, strategically aligned output.
